2022 · Chennai, Tamil Nadu

Body Soda Becomes an Online Trainer

Vadivelu. Chai Kings. A comic strip. 40% direct traffic lift.

The ICP was clear — creators and educators in South India with a skill and the ambition to earn from it. The brief: reach them in Chennai. The insight was simpler than any strategy deck: Chennai runs on chai, any time, any season. Chai Kings, a beloved local chain with high-traffic metro outlets and a growing online order base, became the medium.

For the message, we needed a face Chennai would stop for. No one was more obvious than Vadivelu — Tamil cinema's most beloved comedian, whose "Nesamani" character became a cultural phenomenon during COVID. We used a different character: Body Soda from the film Pokkiri, a martial arts master who is famously, comically, always getting hurt.

We recreated the scene as a comic strip series with a twist: Body Soda gets hurt one too many times, switches to online training, becomes famous, and earns from home without a single bruise. The comic strip ran on Chai Kings tea flasks at select metro-level outlets — high footfall, high online ordering, maximum window visibility — with a QR code linking to a dedicated landing page and a social campaign built around the same narrative.

The result: 40% lift in direct traffic and measurable brand uplift across the Tamil market.

Language Strategy — Observations & Decisions
03
India-specific Observation Localisation Decision Outcome
Pure Tamil UX copy felt foreign even to Tamil speakers — literary terms created friction → 60% Tanglish, 30% pure Tamil, 10% English. Transliterate where needed. "ஆன்லைன் வகுப்பு" not "இணைய வகுப்பு" Higher UX comprehension. Users adopted faster.
Google Ads drove strong India signups from teachers — but conversion flatlined → Support tickets + sales call analysis revealed intent mismatch: users wanted to join as freelance teachers, not build their own platform. Rewrote LP with explicit "What to expect" section. CPL ↓, conversion ↑, MQL→SQL +45%
Hindi activation needed a culturally resonant pain point — not a feature list → Focused on YouTube monetisation complexity and Udemy's high commission model. Hindi-English copy targeting metro creators: Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Lucknow. Higher CTR vs. generic Hindi copy in metro campaigns.
"Zero transaction fees" messaging underperformed in India — creators didn't know they were paying fees elsewhere → Shifted from "we don't charge fees" to "you've been losing money and didn't know it." Made the invisible fee visible first, then positioned TC as the fix. Revenue up 18% in two quarters post-pricing revamp.
SCORM flagged by Indian enterprise prospects in support tickets — deals lost, feature absent → Triangulated: customer reviews → support tickets → sales lost-deal data. Quantified lost deals, built business case, presented to Product team. SCORM pushed to roadmap. Now available as beta feature.
